We're starting a blog. Not because the internet needs more noise, but because beatboxing — as a community and as a craft — deserves a real corner of the web that takes it seriously.

Here's what you'll find here as we ramp up.

## Honest comparisons, not marketing fluff

When we review beatboxing apps, gear, or tools, we'll tell you what they actually do well and where they fall short. When we list our own app alongside competitors, we'll label it clearly — no hiding behind a fake "independent" review.

<Callout variant="info" title="Why we started here">
  The Beatboxx app focuses on helping beatboxers organize ideas, tag techniques, and build routines. But most of the knowledge that makes someone *good* at beatboxing lives in heads, Discord servers, and scattered YouTube videos. This blog is a place to write some of it down.
</Callout>

## Tutorials that respect your time

If a post can teach you something in 4 minutes, it will be 4 minutes long. No 20-paragraph life stories before the recipe.

## Product updates, when they matter

We'll post about Beatboxx app updates here when there's something genuinely worth sharing — a feature that changes how you use the app, not a bug fix for a typo.
